pandas test:
Command: time python3 break_pandas.py
Output: Loaded 161823789 rows in 208.41 seconds
Time: real 3m37.297s

Comparison: CLI (zcat | wc -l) counted the same 24 files in 57.227s (from Phase 1).
pandas took about 3.8x longer just to load the data into memory, even though it didn't crash.
Shows the load-everything-into-memory cost before any analysis even happens.

Note: first run of this only loaded 159,502,523 rows, about 2.3 million short of the CLI
count. Turned out some page titles have literal " characters in them, and pandas was
treating those as CSV quote marks, losing its place partway through parsing. Added
quoting=3 to pd.read_csv() so it treats " as a normal character instead, and after that
the row counts matched exactly.
